# Phase 5: Leverage Agency Referrals

## Context
By Month 7, you have built trust. You are proving that a specialised, external maintenance desk is cheaper and less stressful than trying to hire an internal DevOps/Maintenance engineer. Digital agency owners all talk to each other. Referrals are your strongest growth engine.

## Identifying Network Hubs
Agency founders often sit in exclusive Slack groups, masterminds, or local tech hubs (e.g., BIMA, The Agency Collective). 

## The Referral Ask (The Script)
Do not ask for referrals over email. Do it at the end of a quarterly review video call with a happy client.

*   **You:** "Overall, the infrastructure has been highly stable this quarter. How are things on your end?"
*   **Client:** "Great, my devs love that they don't have to deal with Webpack configs anymore."
*   **You (The Ask):** "I'm glad to hear it. Since we’ve dialed in the process, I have capacity to take over maintenance for one or two more agencies this quarter. Given you sit in [Name of their local agency network/group], do you know any other founders who are complaining about post-launch support or struggling to hire maintenance devs?"

## The "White-Label" Leverage
Remind them that you are non-competitive. Because your model explicitly rejects project builds, they know you will never steal their clients. You only fix the bugs. This makes it incredibly easy for them to recommend you to a peer.

If they make an introduction, treat that lead like gold. Run a free System Health Audit for the referred agency purely on the strength of the introduction.

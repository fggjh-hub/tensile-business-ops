# Phase 5: Hit Month 12 Target

## The Milestone
Month 12 is the final test of the business plan. The target is **£6,000+ MRR**. 
Typically, this looks like 4 clients on Safety Retainers at £1,500/month per covered core system.

## The Reality of £6,000 MRR
Because you have heavily automated dependency updates (Phase 4), and you enforce strict boundaries on feature creep, this £6,000 MRR does not require 40 hours of work per week. It likely requires 15-20 highly focused hours. This realises the core philosophy: low stress, high margin, specialist technical work.

## End of Year UK Tax Planning (2026/2027)
By Month 12, you will accumulate significant cash in the business bank account. You must now meet with an accountant to plan your extraction strategy before your first Corporation Tax bill is due.

1.  **Corporation Tax:** You owe roughly 19% (for small profits) or up to 25% on the profit remaining in the company. *Ensure this is reserved in a separate Xero/Tide tax pot.*
2.  **Dividend Declarations:** You must formally declare dividends to extract the cash personally. Your accountant will structure this to utilise your basic rate tax band effectively.
3.  **Business Expenses:** Ensure you have legally expensed your Virtual Office, PI Insurance, home-office allowance, and tech equipment (the proprietary hardware mentioned in the business plan) against the company profits to legally minimise your tax burden.

## The Next Step
You are now sustainable. At this point, you face the classic business choice:
**A)** Stay solo, keep overheads at near zero, work 15–20 hours a week, and enjoy the profit margins.
**B)** Scale up deliberately by bringing on a second operator to cover more retainers. This means more Audits sold, more covered core systems under management, and a clear split between Sales/Architecture (you) and hands-on maintenance (them). Critically, this does *not* mean adding a third product — the two-product menu stays the same. The only thing that changes is throughput.

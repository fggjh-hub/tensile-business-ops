# Phase 5: Target Funded Startups

## Context
Up until now, you have focused on digital agencies. By Month 8, you should open a secondary lead channel: **Funded Startups**. 

When a startup raises a Seed round or Series A (£1m - £5m), investor pressure dictates that 100% of engineering effort must go into finding product-market fit or building massive new features. However, they usually have an aging MVP that is starting to crack under the pressure, full of technical debt.

## Finding the Target
*   Use **Crunchbase** or **Beauhurst** (UK specific) or simply read **UK Tech News (UKTN)** and **Sifted**.
*   Set up alerts for companies in London, Manchester, or Edinburgh raising "Seed" or "Series A" rounds.
*   Find the CTO or Technical Co-founder via LinkedIn. 

## The "Technical Insurance" Pitch
Startups don't care about "white-labeling"; they care about velocity.

**Cold Email to a Startup CTO:**
> Hi [Name], congrats on the recent seed raise.
> 
> I know right now your board wants your engineers fully focused on scaling the core product, not spending 30% of their week doing dependency upgrades and triaging Sentry errors on the legacy systems.
> 
> I run an independent, UK-based codebase maintenance desk. We stabilise and maintain Rust, React, and Node.js systems that still matter but no longer need full-time attention from your core team.
> 
> Would you be open to a quick chat about offloading some of your technical debt?

## The Advantage
Funded startups have cash. A £1,500/month retainer for one covered core system is a rounding error compared to hiring a £75,000/year DevOps/Maintenance engineer (with employers NI, pension, and equity attached). You are an immediate, cheap, effective plug for their infrastructure leaks.

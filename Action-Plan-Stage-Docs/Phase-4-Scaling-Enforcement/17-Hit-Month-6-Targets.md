# Phase 4: Hit Month 6 Targets

## The Milestone
By Month 6, your goal is a highly stable £3,000 MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue).
*   **£3,000 MRR from Safety Retainers** (2 clients at £1,500/month each).
*   **Ad-Hoc Triage Fixes** remain useful top-up revenue, but they do not count towards MRR.

## Mid-Year Evaluation
At Month 6, you must critically analyse your profitability. 

1.  **Review the Triage Efficiency:** Are you quoting £450 for fixes but spending 8 hours on them? If so, your effective hourly rate is a miserable £56/hr. You must become harsher in your estimates, or push your flat pricing up £150.
2.  **Review Retainer Sizzle:** Are the retainer clients too quiet? It sounds good, but if a client pays £1,500/month and hears nothing for 3 months, they will cancel. Ensure you are sending automated monthly PDF reports detailing exactly how many dependency PRs were merged and what was monitored or patched. Make the invisible work *visible*.
3.  **Are you enjoying it?** The goal of this business model is low stress and clear boundaries. Are clients respecting the 8-hour anti-creep clause? If a specific client is a nightmare, Month 6 is when you invoke the 30-day cancellation clause and fire them.

## Financial Buffer Check
If you are consistently hitting £3k MRR and topping it up with audits or occasional Triage Fixes, the business is now covering basic living expenses. You can slowly begin transitioning off your personal runway savings. Use FreeAgent to run a mock Corporation Tax calculation to ensure you aren't spending the taxman's money.

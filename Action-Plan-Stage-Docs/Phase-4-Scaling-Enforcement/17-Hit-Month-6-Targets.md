# Phase 4: Hit Month 6 Targets

## The Milestone
By Month 6, your goal is a highly stable £3,000 MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue).
*   **£3,000 MRR from Safety Retainers** (2 clients at £1,500/month each, or the equivalent via multiple covered core systems on fewer contracts).
*   **Additional Audit revenue** is welcome as non-recurring top-up income, but it does not count towards MRR and should never become a lifeline.

## Mid-Year Evaluation
At Month 6, critically analyse your profitability and the health of the two-product model.

1.  **Review Audit throughput.** Are you converting Audits into retainers? The target conversion rate is 30–50% of audited systems moving onto a retainer within 60 days of delivery. If you are below 30%, either your audit reports are not making the case persuasively enough, or you are auditing the wrong systems. If you are at 100%, you are not charging enough for the retainer — a few non-conversions is healthy.
2.  **Review retainer sizzle.** Are the retainer clients too quiet? It sounds good, but if a client pays £1,500/month and hears nothing for three months, they will cancel. Ensure the monthly update PDF is going out on time, every time, with real substance — dependency PRs merged, uptime achieved, CVEs reviewed, time spent against the 8-hour cap. Make the invisible work visible.
3.  **Are clients respecting the Monthly Cap?** Clause 2 of the retainer caps active work at 8 hours per covered core system per month. If a specific client consistently pushes past that, invoke the clause in writing and, if necessary, invoke the 30-day notice provision. Month 6 is when you fire the first difficult client — it gets harder after twelve.
4.  **Are you enjoying it?** The goal of this business model is low stress and clear boundaries. If the answer is no, the problem is almost always either an under-priced retainer or a client who hasn't been held to the cap. Fix one or both.

## Financial Buffer Check
If you are consistently hitting £3k MRR and topping it up with Audit revenue, the business is now covering basic living expenses. You can slowly begin transitioning off your personal runway savings. Use FreeAgent or Xero to run a mock Corporation Tax calculation to ensure you aren't spending the taxman's money.

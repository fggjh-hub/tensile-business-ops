# Phase 4: Execute the "Trojan Horse" Upsell

## Context
The System Health Audit is merely the diagnostic tool. It uncovers technical debt that the client generally doesn’t want to fix themselves. The goal of the delivery meeting is to transition them from a one-off audit to a recurring retainer.

## The Delivery Meeting
Do not just email the PDF report. Require a 20-minute Google Meet to "walk through the findings."

### The Presentation Flow:
1.  **Start Positive:** "Overall, the infrastructure is solid. Your team built a great product."
2.  **Present the Red Flags:** "We did find three critical CVEs in your Node modules, and the React version is deeply deprecated."
3.  **The Pivot:** "You can take this PDF, hand it to your developers, and they can probably clear this in a week. However, this pulls them away from building the new features you need to hit your Q3 targets."

### The Upsell Script:
*"Alternatively, instead of derailing your internal roadmap, you can put us on our £1,500/month Safety Retainer for this covered core system. That gives you ongoing dependency updates, security patching, monitoring, monthly reporting, and a guaranteed 8-hour response SLA for production-critical bugs during business hours (Monday to Friday, 09:00–17:00). If you want a second system covered, that sits on a second retainer."*

### Sending the Contract
If they say yes, verbally confirm. End the call. Immediately send the IR35-compliant contract via an e-signature platform (like DocuSign or HelloSign) and set up the recurring mandate in FreeAgent. Do not start work until the first month is paid.

# Phase 3: Launch YunoJuno Presence

## Context
YunoJuno is the premium freelance marketplace in the UK. Unlike Upwork or Fiverr, YunoJuno clients are typically funded startups or established agencies, and the platform enforces 14-day payment terms.

## Profile Strategy
Most developers on YunoJuno list themselves as "Full Stack Developer" or "React Engineer." This is a mistake for your business model because those searches are generated by clients looking to *build* projects. You want to capture the searches of clients who are *panicking* because something is broken.

1.  **The Headline:** 
    *   Instead of "Senior Backend Developer", use: **"Specialist in Legacy Code Rescue & Technical Debt Reduction."**
    *   Alternatively: **"React/Node & Rust Maintenance Specialist."**
2.  **The Bio:**
    *   State clearly what you do *not* do: *"I do not build platforms from scratch. I parachute into existing codebases to fix critical bugs, upgrade legacy dependencies, and stabilise your infrastructure."*
3.  **Skills Tags:**
    *   Ensure you list: `Debugging`, `Refactoring`, `Maintenance`, `Legacy Code`, and your specific tech stack (`Node.js`, `React`, `Rust`, `AWS`, etc.).
4.  **Portfolio Items:**
    *   Do not just show screenshots of pretty websites you built. Show "Before and After" metrics. For example: "Reduced App Load Time by 40% via Query Optimisation" or "Resolved 14 High-Severity Snyk Vulnerabilities in 48 hours for FinTech Client."

## Pitching for Briefs
When a brief drops on YunoJuno that looks like a maintenance nightmare, agencies expect 90% of applicants to reject it. Your cover letter should say: *"Most devs hate maintenance. I specialise in it. I can audit this codebase by Tuesday and begin triaging the critical bugs by Wednesday."*

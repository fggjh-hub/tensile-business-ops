# Phase 1: Procure Insurance

## The Need for Insurance
If you pull down a client's production server while trying to fix a bug, or if you accidentally introduce a security vulnerability during a dependency update, the client could sue your company for lost revenue. 

Because you are a Limited Company, your personal assets are protected, but the company itself needs to survive the claim. Furthermore, B2B clients (especially agencies) will demand proof of insurance before signing contracts.

## Professional Indemnity (PI) Insurance
This is the core insurance you need. It covers legal costs and compensation if a client claims your service was negligent or caused them financial loss.

*   **Target Coverage:** £1,000,000. (Most UK agencies require £1m as a minimum baseline to work with B2B contractors).

## Public Liability (PL) Insurance
Usually bundled with PI. It covers physical injury or property damage (e.g., you spill coffee on a client's server rack). Since you are remote, this is less critical, but often included by default.

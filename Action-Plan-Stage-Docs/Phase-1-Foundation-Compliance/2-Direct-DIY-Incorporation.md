# Phase 1: Direct DIY Incorporation

## Context
Many accountants charge £150-£300 to do this for you. In reality, it takes 15 minutes to do directly with the UK Government online. The government fee was increased recently, and currently sits at £100 for digital incorporation (as of 2026).

## The Exact Process

1. **Go to the Official Portal:** Visit `https://www.gov.uk/register-a-company-online`
2. **Company Name:** Check that your desired name is available and doesn't contain restricted words. Ensure it sounds professional (e.g., "Tensile Infrastructure Ltd"). 
3. **Official Addresses:** 
    * When asked for the **Registered Office Address**, paste the Virtual Address you purchased in step 1.
    * When asked for the **Director's Service Address**, use the same Virtual Address.
    * *Only* when explicitly asked for your "Usual Residential Address" (which is kept hidden from the public by Companies House) do you enter your real home address.
4. **SIC Code (Standard Industrial Classification):** 
    * This tells the government what your business does. 
    * For this business model, use **62020** (Information technology consultancy activities) or **62030** (Computer facilities management activities).
5. **Share Capital:** 
    * Keep it incredibly simple. 
    * Total Shares: 100. 
    * Type: Ordinary. 
    * Value: £1 per share. 
    * You will own 100% of these. This gives you maximum flexibility to pay dividends later.
6. **Persons with Significant Control (PSC):** 
    * You will list yourself as holding 75% or more of the shares and voting rights.
7. **Payment:** 
    * Pay the £100 fee using a personal card (you can reimburse yourself as a business expense once the business bank account is open).

**Verification Time:** Your company is usually incorporated within 24 to 48 hours. You will receive your Certificate of Incorporation via email.

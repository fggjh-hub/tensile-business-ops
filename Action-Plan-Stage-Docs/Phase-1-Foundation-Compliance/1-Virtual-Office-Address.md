# Phase 1: Virtual Office Address

## The Problem
When you incorporate a company in the UK, Companies House requires a **Registered Office Address** (for the company) and a **Director's Service Address** (for you). By default, these are put on the public register, which is easily searchable online. Using your home address is a massive privacy risk.

## The Solution: Virtual Addresses
You can legally use a commercial address provider to host your registered address and forward your mail.

### Recommended Providers (UK 2026)
*   **The Hoxton Mix:** Located in Shoreditch, London. Perfect for a tech/software business looking for a premium address without the cost. Costs around £15-£18/month or roughly £180/year for their core package.
*   **UK Postbox:** Excellent digital-first mail management. Highly flexible.
*   **1st Formations:** They sell address-only packages (you do not need them to form the company for you). Extremely reliable, typically starting around £39/year for just the address components.

### Exact Process
1. Go to the provider's website.
2. Purchase the "Registered Office Address" and the "Director's Service Address" package.
3. You will have to go through an Anti-Money Laundering (AML) check with them. This involves uploading your ID and a selfie.
4. Once verified, they will give you the exact address format (e.g., Your Name, Unit X, 86-90 Paul Street, London, EC2A 4NE).
5. **CRITICAL:** Do not proceed to incorporation until this address is active and approved by the provider.

### Mail Forwarding
Any official mail from HMRC, Companies House, or the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) sent to this address will be opened, scanned, and emailed to you as a PDF on the same day.

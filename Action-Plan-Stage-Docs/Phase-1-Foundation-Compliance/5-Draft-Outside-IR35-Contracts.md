# Phase 1: Draft Outside IR35 Contracts

## The IR35 Threat
IR35 is UK tax legislation. If a client treats you like a disguised employee (you work set hours, you can't send a substitute, they tell you exactly *how* to do your job), HMRC can classify the engagement as "Inside IR35." This destroys your tax advantages and puts liability on the client. Medium/Large UK clients are terrified of IR35. 

Your entire business model relies on being aggressively B2B and **Outside IR35**.

## The Essential Clauses
You cannot just download a generic contractor template. Your contract must actively demonstrate you are an independent business.

1.  **Right of Substitution (RoS):** 
    *   *Clause:* "The Contractor retains the right to provide a suitably qualified substitute to perform the Services. The Contractor remains responsible for paying the substitute."
    *   *Why:* Employees cannot send someone else to do their job. Businesses can.
2.  **Lack of Mutuality of Obligation (MOO):**
    *   *Clause:* "The Client is under no obligation to offer further contracts or services, and the Contractor is under no obligation to accept any such offer."
    *   *Why:* Employees expect steady work. Businesses operate on distinct, scoped deliverables.
3.  **Direction and Control:**
    *   *Clause:* "The Contractor shall determine the method, manner, and means by which the Services are performed."
    *   *Why:* The client can tell you *what* needs fixing (the bug). They cannot tell you *how* to write the code. 
4.  **The Anti-Creep / Scope Boundary:**
    *   *Clause:* "Retainer Support is strictly capped at continuous isolated tasks of up to 8 hours. Any request requiring feature development or exceeding this threshold forms a New Project and is subject to separate negotiation which the Contractor may decline."

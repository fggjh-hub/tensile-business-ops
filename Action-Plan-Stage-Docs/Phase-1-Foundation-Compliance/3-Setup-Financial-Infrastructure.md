# Phase 1: Setup Financial Infrastructure

## Context
Once you have your Certificate of Incorporation, you legally exist as a corporate entity. The immediate next step is separating your finances. Do not let any client money touch your personal account.

## 1. Business Banking (UK 2026)
You need a modern, API-driven bank that integrates with accounting software. Avoid traditional high-street banks (Barclays, HSBC) as application processes are notoriously slow for new startups.

*   **Monzo Business:** Costs around £5/month for their pro tier (which handles invoicing and tax pots natively). Best user experience.

*Action:* Download the app of your chosen bank, upload your ID and Certificate of Incorporation, and secure the account.

## 2. Accounting Software
Because your model relies on **Retainers**, you need software that can send automated recurring invoices.

*   **Xero:** The industry standard. Around £30/month. 

## 3. The Money Flow (Tax Efficiency)
As a sole director in the UK, your standard optimal extraction method is:
1. Pay yourself a small baseline salary (usually up to the National Insurance primary threshold, around £12,570/year) to ensure you secure State Pension qualifying years without paying income tax or NI.
2. Extract the remaining profit as Dividends (which are taxed at lower rates than salary).
*(Note: Always consult an accountant before the end of your first financial year to handle Corporation Tax filings).*

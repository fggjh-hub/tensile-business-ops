# Post-Audit Follow-Up

**Use:** Following up with a client who received an audit report but has not responded or converted to a retainer. Send once, 7–10 days after delivery.

**Send from:** jack@tensileinfrastructure.co.uk
**Subject:** Re: System Health Audit — [Client/System name] — [Date]
*(Reply to the original delivery thread)*

---

## Template — Neutral follow-up

Hi [Name],

Just checking in on the audit report — happy to talk through any of the findings if that would help.

If you've decided to handle the remediation internally, that's completely fine. If ongoing maintenance is something you'd want to hand off, let me know and we can talk through what that looks like.

Jack

## Template — Where the audit surfaced significant issues

Hi [Name],

Wanted to follow up on the report. The [high/critical] findings are the kind of thing that's worth getting in front of sooner rather than later — particularly [the most urgent item, one sentence].

If it would help to talk through the prioritisation or what remediation actually involves, I'm happy to do that. And if you're thinking about ongoing maintenance rather than a one-off fix, that's what the retainer is for.

Jack

---

## Notes

- Send exactly once. Do not chase after this.
- Reply to the original delivery thread so they have the report in context.
- Keep it short — two or three sentences at most.
- Do not apologise for following up.
- Do not re-explain the retainer in detail. They've seen the website and the report.
- If they respond with "not right now", acknowledge it, leave the door open, and move on.
- If they don't respond at all, leave it. One follow-up is enough.

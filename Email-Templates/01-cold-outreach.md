**Send from:** jack@tensileinfrastructure.co.uk
**Subject options:**
- Software maintenance for [Agency Name]
- Keeping [Agency Name]'s live systems in shape
- A question about [Client/Project name]

## Notes

- Keep it short. Three short paragraphs maximum.
- The bracketed line in paragraph two is the most important — it needs to be specific to them, not generic. If you can't fill it in with something real, don't send it.
- Do not mention the retainer in the subject line. Lead with the audit.
- Do not use "I hope this finds you well", "I wanted to reach out", or any similar opener.
- If they have a specific live product you know about, name it in paragraph two.
- Adjust "came across" to match reality — referral, LinkedIn, their website, a conference, etc.
- Use the cost angle variant when the prospect is clearly resource-constrained or developer-focused. Use the standard template when the relationship is more relationship-led or referral-based — the numbers can feel out of place in a warm context.
- The £300/day figure is based on UK average senior developer salary (£60–70k) fully loaded with employer NI and pension. It is conservative and holds nationally — do not adjust it for specific regions.

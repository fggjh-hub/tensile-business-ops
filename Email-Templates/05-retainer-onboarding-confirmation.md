# Retainer Onboarding Confirmation

**Use:** Sent to the client once all onboarding checklist items are complete — monitoring is live, repo access is confirmed, Renovate is running, Snyk is connected, and the agreement is signed.

**Send from:** jack@tensileinfrastructure.co.uk
**Subject:** Tensile Infrastructure — [Client/System name] retainer live from [date]

---

## Template

Hi [Name],

Everything is set up on our end. Here's a quick summary of what's in place:

**Uptime monitoring** is live across [list monitored URLs/services]. You'll hear from us if anything goes down.

**Dependency management** is running via Renovate. You'll start seeing pull requests for dependency updates — these come from the Renovate bot but we review each one before it opens. [Name the agreed process — e.g. "We'll tag you in anything that needs a decision on your side before merging."]

**Vulnerability monitoring** is connected to the repository. We'll flag anything that needs attention as it comes up rather than waiting for the monthly update.

The first monthly update will cover [Month] and will be with you by [date — end of the first full calendar month].

For anything urgent in the meantime, email support@tensileinfrastructure.co.uk and it'll come straight to us. We're on an 8-hour response SLA for critical production issues during business hours (Monday to Friday, 09:00–17:00).

Good to have you on board.

Jack
Tensile Infrastructure
hello@tensileinfrastructure.co.uk

---

## Notes

- Only send this once every item on the Client Onboarding Checklist is ticked. Not before.
- Adjust the monitoring, Renovate, and Snyk paragraphs to reflect what was actually set up.
- Be specific about URLs being monitored — it reassures the client things are genuinely running.
- The first monthly update date should be concrete, not "end of the month".
- Do not over-promise in this email. If something isn't set up yet, say so rather than omitting it.
- File a copy of this email in the client's Drive folder.

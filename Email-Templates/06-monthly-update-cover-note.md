# Monthly Update Cover Note

**Use:** The email body when sending the monthly retainer update. The update document is attached as a PDF.

**Send from:** jack@tensileinfrastructure.co.uk
**Subject:** [Client/System name] — [Month Year] update

---

## Template — Quiet month

Hi [Name],

[Month] update is attached.

Quiet month — [uptime %, e.g. 100% uptime], [X] dependency updates applied, no security issues. Everything in the detail if you want it.

Jack

---

## Template — Standard month with notable items

Hi [Name],

[Month] update is attached.

[2–3 sentences hitting the headlines: uptime, number of updates applied, anything worth flagging. E.g. "Uptime held at 99.97% — one brief incident on the 14th, resolved within 40 minutes, covered in the report. Eight dependency updates applied including a minor version bump to Next.js. One package flagged for next month that'll need a decision before we apply it."]

Let me know if you have questions.

Jack

---

## Template — Month with an incident

Hi [Name],

[Month] update is attached.

Worth flagging upfront: [brief description of the incident — one sentence, plain English]. It's covered in full in the report including what caused it and how it was resolved. [One sentence on current status — e.g. "Everything has been stable since."]

[One sentence on everything else — e.g. "Outside of that, six dependency updates went through without issue and no new vulnerabilities were identified."]

Jack

---

## Notes

- Subject line format is fixed: [Client/System name] — [Month Year] update.
- The cover note should match the month. A quiet month gets a quiet email. Don't dress it up.
- Never send a long cover note — the report is the detail. The email is the headline.
- Send within the first three working days of the new month, covering the previous month.
- Attach the update as a PDF, not a Word document.
- Do not number the updates (e.g. "Monthly Update #4"). Clients don't think of it that way.
- If there was an incident, lead with it in the email — do not bury it at the end.

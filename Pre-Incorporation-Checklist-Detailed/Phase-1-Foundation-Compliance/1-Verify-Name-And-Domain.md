# Pre-Incorporation: Verify Name and Domain

## The Objective
You need to ensure that your business name is legally available to register in the UK, does not infringe on existing trademarks, and has a clean, matching domain name available.

## Step 1: Companies House Verification
1. Go to the active UK Government portal: `https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company-name-availability`
2. Search for your desired name (e.g., "Tensile Infrastructure").
3. Note: Companies House ignores words like "UK", "Services", and "Company" when checking for duplicates. If "Tensile Infrastructure Ltd" exists, you cannot register "Tensile Infrastructure Services Ltd". Ensure it is completely unique.

## Step 2: Trademark Safety Check
Even if Companies House allows the registration, doing a quick trademark check prevents cease-and-desist letters later:
1. Go to the UK Intellectual Property Office (UKIPO) trademark search tool.
2. Search your exact desired name. 
3. If it is trademarked under "Class 42" (Software and Technology Services), pick a new name immediately.

## Step 3: Secure the Domain
Because domains are cheap, buy it immediately before someone else registers it or before you proceed to incorporation.
1. Use **Cloudflare Registrar** (for wholesale pricing) or **Namecheap**.
2. Prioritise a `.co.uk` domain. In the UK B2B services market, a `.co.uk` is highly trusted and expected. If you cannot get `.co.uk`, fallback to `.com`. Avoid `.io`, `.tech`, or `.dev` as they appeal to developers, but you are selling to non-technical agency Founders/CEOs who trust traditional extensions.
3. Pay on your personal debit card. Keep the email receipt safe so you can expense it back to the company later.

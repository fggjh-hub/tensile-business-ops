# Business Plan: Specialised Software Maintenance & Reliability

## 1. Executive Summary
**Mission:** To provide UK SMEs and digital agencies with reliable, specialised software maintenance, bug fixing, and technical debt reduction, operating strictly outside of large-scale project development.
**Business Model:** B2B service provider focusing on high-margin, low-scope tasks: one-off fixes, system audits, and recurring maintenance retainers.
**Legal Structure:** Private Limited Company (Ltd) registered in England and Wales.

## 2. Company Description
Operating remotely from the North West, the business acts as a "Technical Insurance Policy" for companies that have existing software but lack the internal resources for dedicated maintenance. By explicitly refusing large feature development or project work, the business guarantees fast response times and high availability for critical fixes, remaining firmly Outside IR35. The core philosophy is protecting existing assets rather than building new ones.

## 3. Services & Pricing Structure
The central principle is selling outcomes and availability, not billable hours. 

* **The System Health Audit (The Lead Magnet): £500 (Fixed Flat-Rate)**
    * A comprehensive review of a client's codebase focusing on security vulnerabilities, outdated dependencies, and performance bottlenecks. Delivered as a 3-5 page technical report with actionable recommendations.
* **The Safety Retainer: £1,500/month per covered core system**
    * Ongoing dependency management, security patching, uptime monitoring, and a monthly written update.
    * Guaranteed 8-hour SLA response for critical production breaks during business hours (Monday to Friday, 09:00–17:00 UK time).
    * Each retainer covers one named production application or service, plus the datastore and infrastructure directly supporting it.
    * Separate apps, admin portals, marketing sites, APIs, worker services, and mobile apps require separate coverage.
    * *Strict Anti-Creep Clause:* Any requests exceeding 8 continuous hours of development are automatically rejected and require separate negotiation, which the business reserves the right to decline.
The business has two products and only two products. Ad-hoc bug-fixing work is not offered. Clients who present with a one-off problem are directed either to the £500 audit (if the issue is symptomatic of broader drift) or declined politely. This keeps the operating model simple, keeps IR35 positioning clean, and avoids the low-margin treadmill of single-ticket fire-fighting.

## 4. Market & Competitor Analysis (UK 2026)
* **Target Market 1: Digital Agencies.** Agencies frequently build platforms but struggle to maintain them profitably once launched. 
* **Target Market 2: Funded Startups (Seed/Series A).** Teams focused entirely on product-market fit and new features, often leaving a trail of technical debt and unmanaged infrastructure.
* **Competitive Advantage:** Most freelance software developers want to build systems from scratch and dislike maintenance. By exclusively embracing maintenance, the business captures a severely underserved niche. Clear Outside IR35 positioning makes procurement frictionless for medium-sized UK companies.

## 5. Marketing & Sales Strategy
* **Platform Presence:** Maintain a highly optimised profile on YunoJuno, targeting "Bug Fixing" and "Legacy Maintenance" to capture high-intent, vetted UK clients.
* **Agency White-Labelling:** Direct cold outreach to founders of design and marketing agencies, offering a white-label maintenance desk for their existing client catalogue.
* **The "Trojan Horse" Audit:** Use the low-cost System Health Audit as the primary sales tool to demonstrate competence, subsequently upselling the client to a Safety Retainer for that covered core system if ongoing maintenance is warranted.

## 6. Operational Plan
* **Technology & Tools:** * *Accounting:* FreeAgent or Xero (for automated recurring invoices).
    * *Banking:* Digital business account (e.g., Tide, Monzo Business).
    * *Communication:* Slack Connect (for retainer clients) and automated ticketing via Linear or Jira.
* **Risk Management:** £1m Professional Indemnity (PI) Insurance maintained at all times. Contracts strictly enforce the "Right of Substitution" and "No Mutuality of Obligation" to ensure Outside IR35 compliance.
* **Hardware & Setup:** All work is conducted on proprietary hardware to maintain clear B2B separation from clients.

## 7. Financial Plan
* **Initial Capital:** Funded by existing personal financial buffer, requiring zero external debt.
* **Overheads:** Minimal (PI Insurance, accounting software, domain/hosting, communication tools). Estimated at < £100/month.
* **Revenue Goals (Year 1):** * *Months 1-3:* Secure incorporation, 3 Audits, and 1 Retainer client.
    * *Month 6 Target:* £3,000 MRR via 2 Retainers, supplemented by ad-hoc Audit revenue as non-recurring income.
    * *Month 12 Target:* £6,000+ MRR via 4 Retainers, allowing full transition from the financial buffer to sustainable, low-stress profitability.

# 2026 Action Plan: Specialised Software Maintenance

This document outlines the exact, step-by-step sequence of events required to execute your business plan and achieve sustainable profitability in the UK by the end of 2026. It has been updated with highly specific, actionable details so you can execute without second-guessing.

## Phase 1: Foundation & Compliance (Month 1)
*Goal: Legally establish the business, protect against liability, and ensure robust Outside IR35 status.*

1. **Keep Your Home Address Private (Virtual Office):** 
    *   Do not register with your home address, as it will become public permanently on Companies House. 
    *   Before registering the company, buy a "Registered Office Address" and a "Director’s Service Address" package from a virtual office provider like **The Hoxton Mix**, **UK Postbox**, or **1st Formations**. This costs roughly £15-£40 per year. They will scan and email you official HMRC and Companies House mail.
2. **Direct DIY Incorporation:** 
    *   You do not need a third-party accountant to form the company. Go directly to **Gov.uk/register-a-company**.
    *   You will need to pay the standard Companies House standard incorporation fee (currently £100 as of 2026). 
    *   When asked, paste the new Virtual Address you just bought for *both* the Company Registered Office and your details as Director.
    *   Set yourself as the sole Director and sole Shareholder holding 100 ordinary shares at £1 each. Choose SIC Code **62020** (Information technology consultancy activities) or **62030** (Computer facilities management activities).
3. **Setup Financial Infrastructure:** 
    *   Once you have the Incorporation Certificate, immediately open a business bank account. **Tide**, **Monzo Business**, or **Starling** are the fastest, 100% digital options.
    *   Link the bank account to **FreeAgent** or **Xero**. Both have excellent automation for recurring retainer invoices.
4. **Procure Insurance:** 
    *   Use a modern, tech-friendly, UK broker like **With Jack** (highly recommended for developers) or **Hiscox**. 
    *   Purchase £1m Professional Indemnity (PI) Insurance. Do not engage clients without this. 
5. **Draft Outside IR35 Contracts:** 
    *   Use a recognised contractor template provider like **Qdos Contractor** or get a legal review.
    *   **Specific Clauses:** Ensure your contract explicitly states: (1) Right of Substitution (you can send someone else and pay them), (2) No Mutuality of Obligation, (3) Delivery based on outcomes/tasks, not hours, and (4) The Anti-Creep Scope Clause (auto-reject requests exceeding 8 hours of continuous work).

## Phase 2: Operations & Productisation (Month 1-2)
*Goal: Set up the systems required to deliver the services efficiently and professionally.*

6. **Establish Your Professional 'Desk':** 
    *   Buy a professional domain (e.g., via Cloudflare) and set up a **Google Workspace** email (e.g., `hello@yourcompany.co.uk`). 
    *   Set up a free **Slack Workspace** that you fully control. You will invite future retainer clients into private channels via Slack Connect.
    *   Set up **Linear** or **Jira** for ticketing. Create an email bridge so clients can literally email `support@yourcompany.co.uk` and it auto-generates a ticket.
7. **Productise the "System Health Audit" (The Lead Magnet):** 
    *   Standardise the deliverables so it takes you less than 3 hours to perform. 
    *   **Tools to run:** `npm audit` or `yarn audit` for dependencies, **Snyk CLI** for security scanning, **Google Lighthouse** for frontend metrics.
    *   Create a clean, branded PDF template using Canva or Notion containing four sections: Security Vulnerabilities, Outdated Dependencies, Performance Bottlenecks, and Actionable Steps.
8. **Define the Triage Process:** 
    *   Document your process: When a client emails a bug, you reply asynchronously within 24 hours acknowledging receipt, review the complexity, and quote them the fixed Triage Fee (£500). Work only begins *after* they confirm via email.

## Phase 3: Go-To-Market & Initial Pipeline (Months 2-3)
*Goal: Launch the offering to the primary target markets and secure the first paying clients.*

9. **Launch YunoJuno Presence:** 
    *   Sign up for YunoJuno. Your headline should NOT be "Full Stack Developer". It should be "Specialist in Legacy Code Rescue & Technical Debt Reduction."
    *   List specific debugging, refactoring, and dev-ops skills.
10. **Build the Agency Target List:** 
    *   Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator to find founders, Technical Directors, or Managing Directors of UK agencies sized 10-50 people.
11. **Execute Cold Outreach (The Email Framework):** 
    *   *Subject:* Handling your post-launch bugs
    *   *Body:* Keep it short. "Hi [Name], I noticed [Agency] ships great web platforms. Most agencies struggle to profitably maintain client sites post-launch because developers want to build new features. I run a UK-based white-label maintenance desk. We strictly handle bug fixes and dependency management. Do you have any launched clients currently causing infrastructure headaches?"
12. **Sell the First Audits:** 
    *   If they express interest, pitch the £400-£600 System Health Audit as a zero-risk trial of your team's competence.
13. **Hit Month 3 Targets:** Complete 3 System Health Audits and convert at least 1 into a monthly Safety Retainer.

## Phase 4: Scaling & Boundary Enforcement (Months 4-6)
*Goal: Turn audits into recurring revenue and aggressively protect your time.*

14. **Execute the "Trojan Horse" Upsell:** 
    *   At the end of every System Health Audit, jump on a 20-minute video call to walk through the PDF. Say, *"You can hand this to your developers, or you can put us on a £1,500/month Safety Retainer for this covered core system. That gives you ongoing dependency updates, security patching, monitoring, monthly reporting, and an 8-hour response during business hours if the production system breaks."*
15. **Enforce the Anti-Creep Clause:** 
    *   You will inevitably get asked: *"Can you just build this quick feature?"* **You must say no.** Reply: *"Our SLA covers maintenance, stability, and bug fixing. We don't take on new feature development, as it jeopardises our emergency response times."*
16. **Automate Retainer Delivery:** 
    *   For clients on the Safety Retainer, install **Dependabot** or **Renovate** into their GitHub/GitLab repositories. Have updates auto-create PRs. You review and merge them weekly. Turn a manual chore into high-margin automated value.
17. **Hit Month 6 Targets:** Achieve £3,000 MRR from 2 retainers. Treat any ad-hoc Triage Fixes as non-recurring top-up revenue rather than part of MRR.

## Phase 5: Profitability & Full Transition (Months 7-12)
*Goal: Reach the financial targets required to rely entirely on the business without high stress.*

18. **Leverage Agency Referrals:** 
    *   Ask your first successful partner: *"Do you know any other local agency owners drowning in post-launch support?"*
19. **Target Funded Startups (Secondary Market):** 
    *   Search Crunchbase or UK Tech News for recently funded (Seed) UK startups. Reach out to the CTO: *"Your engineers are focused on building features for your Series A. We handle dependency upgrades and production maintenance on older systems so they can stay focused on the core product."*
20. **Hit Month 12 Target:** 
    *   Reach £6,000+ MRR. You are now running a sustainable, highly targeted productised service.
